## Why Astro?

When I redesigned my portfolio I had one hard requirement: **zero JS shipped to the browser unless I explicitly opt in.**

Astro nails this with its island architecture. The whole page renders to static HTML at build time. Components that need interactivity get the `client:load` or `client:visible` directive and hydrate independently.

## The result

- Lighthouse score: 100 / 100 / 100 / 100 on desktop
- First Contentful Paint under 400 ms
- Zero layout shift

## What I learned

The `.astro` component format is a superset of HTML. Frontmatter runs at build time (Node), the template is Astro JSX-ish, and styles are scoped by default. Coming from Vue it felt immediately familiar.

The one gotcha: `astro:page-load` fires after each view-transition navigation, not just the initial load. Wire all your JS setup there and it just works.
